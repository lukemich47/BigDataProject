Visualizing Riyadh Conversations and Movement

The City

Riyadh is the capital and largest city in Saudi Arabia, with a population of over 7.3 million. Residents are largely Saudi, although there is also a significant contingent of foreign workers. The government plans a new transit system, so we are looking at traffic patterns throughout the city.

The Idea

We're using python code to grab large datasets from Twitter. By collecting publicly accessible tweet times, locations, and content, we can map social media activity within the city, watch it evolve over time, and learn what topics people are talking about.

We expect to find dips in activity around prayer times, which our visualization should make clear. We are also curious to uncover any geographic or temporal patterns to tweets with religious content, or other categories. Our visualization concept is inspired by this visualization of spending categories, where data points appear and disappear in time, with each color matching up to a category.

Finally, if we have time: since we have also access to CDR flow data, we would like to visualize those movement patterns over time.
